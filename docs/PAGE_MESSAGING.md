# ISJ v4 — Page Messaging Guide

Brief, guiding. Not a script. Each page should feel like ISJ, not like a template.

Themes that run through the whole site:
- Every child is known by name
- Teachers who came here by choice, from the best schools
- Children learn how to think, not what to think
- Risk-taking, confidence, joy in learning
- British curriculum, Jakarta location — that's the product, say it clearly
- Show, don't list. One vivid detail beats three bullet points.

## Jakarta Life Insights — Standard Brief

All Jakarta Life insight articles follow this template.

**Job:** Help expat and internationally-minded families make sense of life in Jakarta. Position ISJ as the trusted local expert without making the article about ISJ.
**Audience:** Families considering relocating to Jakarta, or recently arrived. International and Indonesian.
**Tone:** Factual, specific, direct. No fluff. No marketing tone. Write like a knowledgeable friend who lives in Jakarta, not like a school brochure.
**ISJ mentions:** One natural contextual mention per article where genuinely relevant. Never forced. Footer CTA handles conversion.
**Competitor schools:** Can be named when factually relevant (locations, context). Do not evaluate or compare them.
**SEO:** Each article has a primary keyword. Use it naturally in the title, first paragraph, and one H2.
**Structure:** Hero image → summary prop (lead paragraph) → H2 sections → natural internal link to related ISJ content at end.
**No:** bullet-point TL;DRs, callout boxes, testimonial components, blockquotes, em dashes, exam board names, ISJ self-referencing as "we/our" in body copy.
